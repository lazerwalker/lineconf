LineConf East 2016

Sunday, October 23, 2016 | New York, NY

LineConf

New York City, also known as "The City That Always Waits", is home to so many great lines.

We're a group of line connoiseurs who curated a day of waiting in some of NYC's best lines, paired along with talks on line-related topics like: Lines of Code, Queuing Theory, Lines of Poetry, Assembly Lines, Line Dancing, Line Art, Professional Line Waiting, and more. We invite you to explore lines with us!

You're not required to purchase anything at the end of the line. We encourage participants to bring snacks and water. We'll also have a few folding stools available.

Feel free to invite friends or come up with a topic you'd like to speak about.

Tentative Schedule

Part of waiting in lines is being forced to confront uncertainty and our inability to fully control our destiny. As such, this schedule is tentative!

If you're arriving mid-LineConf, text one of the organizers for our whereabouts or get the Line app and join our group LineConf East.

10:00: Meet at Long Lines Building (33 Thomas St.) for Registration and Line Wobbler

10:15 Conference begins! Receive lanyards, and cronuts! Q&A with our keynote speaker: Ja Nae W. A professional line waiter from Task Rabbit with 100% positive reviews.

10:30: Walking tour of assembly lines + Punchlines: Jokes by Jaime Sunwoo

11:00: Wait in line at Russ and Daughters + presentation from John Earle -- Around the Bloc: Waiting in Lines in the Soviet Union

11:30: Visit the Lowline Lab

12:30: Wait in line at the Trader Joe's Union Square + presentation from Elaine Kung on her Lifelines drawing project

1:30: Walk the Highline + presentation about designing hotlines from Mike Walker + musical performance Richard Aufrichtig

2:30: Send postcards to our favorite establishments with good lines at the 34th St. Post Office

3:30: Wait in the TKTS Times Square line + attend the play "Get Ready For One Hundred Owls in a Room" By S'Gonna Be Weird/IRYFOYF featuring Rowan Magee, Ben Katz, Shayna Strype and Emma Wiseman

4:30: Take the Q(ueue) Line to wait at Shake Shack in Madison Square Park + Hard Lines: an open mic for stories about line experiences

5:30: Wait for a table at La Birreria, Eataly's rooftop beer garden (after-party)
